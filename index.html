St Georges Sailing Club CIC

Junior sailing with a strong community spirit.

St Georges Sailing Club offers a welcoming way for young people and families connected to St George’s to enjoy sailing together. We operate through St Georges Sailing Club Community Interest Company and sail at Bury Lake Young Mariners in Rickmansworth.

At a glance

  • Friendly, parents-run sailing community.
  • Sessions typically take place on Sundays during the sailing season.
  • Based at Bury Lake Young Mariners.
  • Parent helpers are warmly encouraged to get involved.

A club built around young sailors, families and confidence on the water.

We aim to create an enjoyable environment where pupils can develop sailing skills, spend time outdoors and build independence, teamwork and resilience. The club has grown out of a long-standing school sailing community and now operates as its own CIC.

Community-led

Our sessions are supported by parents and volunteers who help the club run smoothly and keep the atmosphere friendly and inclusive.

Practical learning

Young sailors learn by doing, building confidence on the water through regular participation and shared experience.

Outdoor adventure

Sailing offers fresh air, responsibility, teamwork and fun, all in a setting that encourages steady growth and enjoyment.

How sailing sessions work

We sail from Bury Lake Young Mariners and organise sessions as a parents-run club. Families who are interested can get in touch to find out more about membership, communication arrangements and how the season is managed.

What to expect

  • A welcoming club environment for young sailors and supporting families.
  • Sunday sailing through the season, subject to conditions and club arrangements.
  • Clear communications for members about sessions and logistics.
  • A focus on safe, enjoyable participation and progressive confidence-building.

Club administration

  • The club operates through St Georges Sailing Club Community Interest Company.
  • Member communications and club administration are managed digitally.
  • Details for joining and seasonal updates are shared directly with interested families.
  • Please contact us for the latest information on the current season.

Parent helpers are very welcome.

The club depends on the energy and goodwill of parents. Whether you can help regularly or only occasionally, practical support makes a real difference.

Ways to help

  • Supporting session organisation on the day.
  • Helping younger sailors feel settled and confident.
  • Assisting with communications and club administration.
  • Contributing to the friendly, community-led spirit of the club.

Interested in getting involved?

Even a small amount of help can be valuable. If you would like to support the club as a parent helper, please get in touch and we will explain where help is most useful.
